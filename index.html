Rabbit Farming Tips

Housing & Shelter

  • Provide 0.5-1 m² space per rabbit
  • Ensure good ventilation but avoid drafts
  • Use wire mesh flooring for hygiene
  • Keep shelter dry and protected from elements
  • Provide nesting boxes for pregnant does
  • Clean hutches regularly to prevent diseases

Feeding & Nutrition

  • Provide fresh, clean water daily
  • Feed high-quality pellets as base diet
  • Supplement with fresh greens and hay
  • Avoid sudden changes in diet
  • Provide mineral blocks for essential nutrients
  • Monitor food intake for health indicators

Health & Care

  • Vaccinate against common diseases
  • Check for signs of illness daily
  • Maintain proper hygiene in living areas
  • Handle rabbits gently to reduce stress
  • Trim nails regularly if not wearing down naturally
  • Isolate sick animals immediately

Breeding & Reproduction

  • Breed does at 5-6 months of age
  • Gestation period is 28-31 days
  • Provide privacy for nesting mothers
  • Wean kits at 4-6 weeks old
  • Keep breeding records for management
  • Allow does to rest between litters

These tips are based on successful rabbit farming practices. For personalized advice, contact our experts.
